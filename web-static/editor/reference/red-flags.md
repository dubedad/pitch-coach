# Reference — the common pre-seed deck-killers

The recurring mistakes that make investors pass. Use this to spot issues fast and
to name the investor-side read. Grouped by category. The first few in each group
are the most fatal.

## Narrative failures

1. **Buried lede / solution before problem.** Product or vision leads; the problem
   shows up late or never. Investor read: "solution in search of a problem."
2. **No 'why now.'** Nothing explains why the window just opened. Investor read:
   "why hasn't this been done, and what stops an incumbent?"
3. **Vitamin, not painkiller.** The problem is a nice-to-have. Investor read: "nobody
   will pay to fix this."
4. **Feature tour instead of insight.** The solution slide lists features rather than
   the one insight that makes them win. Investor read: "no unfair advantage."
5. **Vague one-liner.** Can't tell what the company does in the first 10 seconds.
   Investor read: unclear thinker; passes on momentum alone.

## Market failures

6. **Top-down TAM theater.** "$X billion market, we'll take 1%." Investor read:
   "hasn't done the real math." Unfalsifiable number = no number.
7. **No bottoms-up logic.** No customers × price build-up. Can't tell if the reachable
   market is venture-scale.
8. **Market too small (honestly).** Even bottoms-up, the ceiling can't return a fund.
9. **Confusing TAM/SAM/SOM.** Numbers that don't nest or contradict each other. Read:
   sloppy thinking.

## Traction failures

10. **Vanity metrics.** Signups, impressions, "pipeline," LOIs presented as demand.
    Read: "no real, paying pull."
11. **Unqualified numbers.** "40% MoM growth" with no base; "10,000 users" with no
    activation. Read: assume the least flattering interpretation.
12. **Pilots/logos dressed as revenue.** Enterprise logos that are unpaid pilots.
    Read: "zero paying customers, possibly no demand."
13. **Leading with the wrong number.** Burying the one genuinely strong metric under
    weaker ones.

## Team failures

14. **No founder-market fit.** "Passionate about X." Read: enthusiasm ≠ an unfair
    reason to win.
15. **Missing domain or operating credibility.** No evidence they've lived the problem
    or shipped anything.
16. **'Advisors: TBD' / placeholder team.** Signals earliness on relationships and
    execution.
17. **Too many co-founders, unclear roles**, or key gap (e.g., all business, no one
    who can build).

## Ask failures

18. **Round size not tied to milestones.** "$1.5M" with no use of funds or milestone.
    Read: can't judge if the number is right.
19. **Ask that doesn't match stage/milestone.** Raising too little to hit the next
    round's bar, or too much for the traction shown.
20. **No use-of-funds specificity.** "For growth and hiring." Read: hasn't planned
    the next 18 months.

## How to use this list

- Map each real weakness to the closest killer, then translate it into the
  investor-side read (Rule 3).
- Not every deck has all of these; don't manufacture problems to fill the list.
- If several are present, rank by the severity order in `rules.md` and surface only
  the top 3 as deal-killers.
