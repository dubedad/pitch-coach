# A credible "why now"

Investors don't fund problems that have existed forever with no clock on them. A strong
"why now" names a recent shift that makes this the right moment — a new technology,
a regulation change, a cost curve that just crossed, a behavior that just became normal.

- Weak: "This market is huge and growing." (No clock.)
- Strong: "X became possible only after [specific recent change], and incumbents are
  structurally slow to react because [reason]."

Coaching question to ask a founder: *"What changed in the last 18 months that makes now
the moment — and why couldn't this have been built three years ago?"*
