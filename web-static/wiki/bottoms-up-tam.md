# Bottoms-up market sizing beats top-down TAM

"It's a $50B market and we only need 1%" tells an investor nothing — it's a top-down
claim anyone can make. A bottoms-up number shows you understand your actual customer.

Build it from the unit up:
- (number of reachable customers) × (what one pays per year) = your real, defensible TAM.
- Then show how you reach them and what it costs to acquire one.

Coaching question: *"Forget the analyst report — how many specific buyers can you name or
count, and what does one of them pay you in year one?"*
