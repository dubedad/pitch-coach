# Reference — what pre-seed investors actually look for

Use this to write the "investor-side read" for any weakness. It tells you what the
person on the other side of the deck is pattern-matching for.

## The 90-second cold read

A pre-seed investor sees dozens of decks a week. The first pass is fast and
skeptical. In roughly 90 seconds they're trying to answer:

1. **What do they do?** (Title/one-liner — if unclear here, momentum is already lost.)
2. **Is the problem real and painful?** (Vitamin vs. painkiller.)
3. **Why this team?** (Founder-market fit.)
4. **Is there any evidence of pull?** (Traction, even tiny.)
5. **Could this be huge?** (Market.)

If any of those reads as a clear "no," they pass — regardless of how polished the
rest is. Your job is to catch the "no" before the investor does.

## What pre-seed investors pattern-match for

- **Founder-market fit.** At pre-seed there's little product and less revenue, so
  they're largely betting on the *founders.* Evidence you have lived this problem
  or have an unfair insight beats any metric.
- **A painkiller, not a vitamin.** Problems people will pay to remove *now*, not
  "nice to have" improvements.
- **Evidence of pull.** Not scale — *pull.* Ten users who love it and pay beats
  10,000 dormant signups. They want proof someone actually wants this.
- **A credible "why now."** A reason the window just opened. Without it, the
  implicit question is "why hasn't this been done, and why won't an incumbent do
  it?"
- **Venture-scale potential.** The math has to support a fund-returning outcome,
  which means bottoms-up market logic, not a "1% of a huge market" hand-wave.
- **Clarity of thought.** The deck is a proxy for how the founder thinks. A muddled
  deck signals muddled thinking; a sharp, honest one signals a sharp operator.

## Pre-seed vs. seed vs. Series A (calibrate to this)

| | Pre-seed | Seed | Series A |
|---|---|---|---|
| Betting mostly on | Founders + insight | Early product + signal | Repeatable growth |
| Traction expected | Pull, even tiny; sometimes none | Early revenue / strong usage | Real revenue, efficient growth |
| Market proof | Bottoms-up logic | Same + early validation | Proven demand |
| Fatal if missing | Founder-market fit, real problem | Evidence of pull | Unit economics / growth efficiency |

If a deck is held to the wrong bar — e.g., apologizing for no revenue at pre-seed,
or claiming "traction" that's really just seed-stage table stakes — flag the
mismatch.

## The honesty premium

Investors reward decks that name their own risks and weaknesses credibly. A deck
that pretends everything is perfect reads as naive or dishonest. If a founder is
papering over an obvious gap, the strongest note is often: *address this head-on
rather than hoping the investor doesn't notice — they will, and the cover-up costs
you more than the gap.*
